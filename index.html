Coffee Basics

Coffee is the one thing that keeps the world moving. Whether it's a Starbucks, your local hipster coffee shop, or making your own coffee in a coffee maker at home, you can always rely on that cup of joe to wake you up or keep you day going. Coffee comes in three different types of roasts: light, medium, and dark. Sometimes coffee is advertised as an espresso roast, but do not be fooled because that is just a very dark roast that can still be enjoyed regularly. There are many ways to make coffee such as a standard plug in coffee maker, a french press, a chimex for pour over coffee, keurig machines, and if you're feeling extra creative you can use an aeropress. All of these methods will get you the cup you will enjoy.

Choose Your Preferred Roast

Light Roast

If you are looking for a more flavorful and stronger coffee, look no further than our light roast coffee beans. Our light roast has a fruity, winey, more acidic taste than your average cup of coffee. These tend to be any coffee snobs favorite choice.

Medium Roast

If you are looking for a middle ground coffee roast with both flavor and body, our medium roast is for you. This roast has subtle hints of flavors such as cocoa, chocolate and caramel. If you use a French Press, this roast is a great choice.

Dark Roast

This coffee roast has a dark brown color. The flavor offers a very low acidic taste and tends to have less caffeine. This roast is great for an espresso or any drink that can be mixed with steamed milk, stuch as a cappucino or a latte.
